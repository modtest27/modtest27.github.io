    Care info

    Sun: The Philodendron Cordatum prefers bright and indirect light, though they also thrive in medium to low light conditions. Leaf colors will dull in lower light.

    Water: Keep soil slightly moist and water once the soil has dried about halfway. They are forgiving but appreciate consistent waterings. Reduce frequency during fall and winter.

    Air: Mimic a tropical environment with temperatures between 65º-85º and a high moisture content.

    Provide a regular boost of humidity by:
    • Adding a humidifier nearby
    • Creating a pebble tray that lives underneath the pot
    • Placement in a bathroom or kitchen, if lighting is suitable
    • Misting the leaves 2-4x per week with non-tap water

    About The Philodendron Cordatum

    Easy-going and fast-growing, the Philodendron Cordatum can hang, trail, or climb under a variety of conditions. Sure to add a tropical feel to your space, these deep-green beauties are a must-have for any plant lover.

    Philodendron Cordatum care guide

    FAQs

    Is This Plant Pet Safe?

    Keep out of reach from curious pets. These are toxic when ingested.

    What Health Benefits Does This Plant Provide?

    Active exposure to and care for plants has many proven wellbeing benefits, such as: 

    • Lower stress levels

    •Improved mood

    • Superior mental clarity and focus

    Houseplants are excellent air purifiers. They cleanse the air of indoor toxins, produce oxygen, and their humidifying capabilities will help you sleep + breathe better. Keep their leaves free of dust for optimal photosynthesis and oxygen production.

    Plant Lifecycle

    The Philodendron Cordatum is a lifelong plant, growing over 6 feet with proper care. They can be trained to climb or trimmed to maintain a smaller size. Stem cuttings are easily propagated.

    For extended life and health, use a well-draining soil and planter. Fertilize during the growing season
